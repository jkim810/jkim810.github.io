    • WHO I AM

    -Personal Introduction-

    I was born in south Korea in August 10th 1994. My father is Byeong Kwan Kim and my mother is Seung Hee Kim. As my father has been working in various countries over the past twenty years, I have been living in an international environment, such as China, Egypt, Czech Republic, for more than half of my life. I have studied in English speaking international schools for more than eight years, and therefore I am proficient at English. As a consequence of studying with people from all around the globe from early age, I have developed a better understanding of internatioinal society and cultural tolerance.

    -Career as an Engineer-

    Ever since I was a fifth grader, I knew that I was an inborn engineer. The way that I learned and understood things naturally clued at the existence of my engineering soul. In all instances approaching problems, the point of views that I took to comprehend the world converged to the fact that I was meant to be a problem solver. I never had to go through the pain of deciding what I want to become, because I knew what I wanted.

    Unfortunately, this might not be the best. Because I did not spend as much time as other people did deciding what I wanted to become, I might have had a less of a philosophy in why I wanted to become an engineer. Regardless, I believe that I am no less of an electrical engineer because I spent a lot of time in deciding what kind of engineer that I wanted to become. As a matter of fact, I used to be a mechanical engineering major until my first year in The Cooper Union. I want to become an electrical engineer because I love math, I love programming, I love computers, and I love solving problems.
